# Terms of Service Placeholder

> **No terms are supplied by this repository.** This is a release-blocking checklist, not a contract, legal advice, warranty, privacy promise, or grant of rights. The owner and qualified legal reviewer must provide and approve actual terms before public service operation or distribution where terms are required.

## Required Owner/Legal Decisions

- Contracting legal entity, product/service name, address, and notices/contact details.
- Who may use the service, age/authority requirements, account registration, and organizational accounts.
- Acceptable use, prohibited abuse, automated access, rate limits, and enforcement/appeal process.
- User responsibility for vault authorization, backups, reviewing diffs, third-party MCP/model hosts, and imported content.
- Service scope and distinction among local open-source components, hosted remote service, companion plugin, and preserved greeting prototype.
- Fees, taxes, trials, renewals, cancellation, and refunds, if any.
- Ownership and licenses for software, documentation, user vault content, feedback, trademarks, and generated output.
- Confidentiality and how service providers process content; align with the approved privacy notice and data-processing terms.
- Availability, maintenance, support, beta features, changes, suspension, termination, data export, and account deletion.
- Security incident handling and vulnerability reporting.
- Warranty disclaimers, limitation of liability, indemnity, force majeure, and statutory consumer rights.
- Governing law, venue/dispute process, arbitration/class-action language where lawful, and regional addenda.
- Third-party services/dependencies and their terms.
- Effective date, version history, acceptance mechanism, and material-change notice.

## Engineering Facts Legal Text Must Not Contradict

- Local HTTP is unauthenticated and intended only for loopback development.
- The remote gateway package is not a production service and the repository has no production account/deletion implementation.
- Note content can be sent to an MCP/model host when a user invokes tools.
- Remote payloads are protected by TLS, not application-level end-to-end encryption.
- Device/vault credentials are stored in Obsidian plugin data, which is not hardware-backed.
- The default credential lifetimes are 90 days for device identity and 30 days for vault authorization; expiry requires re-pairing.
- Workbench uses trash rather than public hard delete, but users still need independent backups.
- No repository license file currently defines public source distribution rights.

The approved terms and approved [privacy notice](privacy.md) must identify their owner, approval date, effective date, and version. Remove the placeholder label only after legal approval and implementation of every promised operational process.
